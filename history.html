The Eras of Rome

Rome wasn't built in a day, nor did it remain the same. Its history is broadly divided into three monumental chapters:

  • The Monarchy (753 BC – 509 BC): The legendary beginnings started by Romulus.
  • The Republic (509 BC – 27 BC): A complex system of governance led by senatorial decree and consuls.
  • The Empire (27 BC – 476 AD): Initiated by Augustus Caesar, establishing Pax Romana across the western world.

Pax Romana & Beyond

During the golden age of Pax Romana, trade flourished, roads connected the known universe, and arts thrived. Philosophers like Marcus Aurelius reminds us of the stoic resolve that kept the empire grounded during challenging times.
